title = 'Thirty Six'

Today I stand where circles meet,
Where numbered years and heartbeats greet;
Not merely older—more aware,
Of hidden patterns everywhere.

For life first learned to write in **three**,
Its secret script of destiny;
Each tiny codon, softly spun,
A whispered verse: *Become. Be one.*

And from the humble number **six**
Rose carbon's quiet, ancient bricks;
Six steadfast sparks in every flame,
The dust of stars from which we came.

In honeycomb and crystal snow,
The hexagons of nature grow;
They teach that beauty often starts
Where order patiently shapes hearts.

Then came the square of six fulfilled—
**Thirty-six**, where dreams are built.
Not haste, nor youth's impatient fire,
But roots grown deep and climbing higher.

The sum of steps, from one through eight,
A mountain shaped by hands of fate;
Each joy, each scar, each hope once small,
Has found its place inside them all.

This body's warmth—its quiet grace—
Still keeps a faithful, living place;
A gentle fire, serene, alive,
Enough to love, enough to strive.

And somewhere, ancient voices say,
There walk **thirty-six** who light the way;
Unknown to crowns, untouched by fame,
Yet all the world endures by them.

Perhaps their gift is simply this:
To turn each ordinary kiss,
Each patient word, each offered hand,
Into the strength on which we stand.

So let this birthday not proclaim
Another number, date, or name.
Let it remind my soul instead
How every living thread is bred:

From **three**, the language life first spoke;
From **six**, the star-born bond it woke;
From **thirty-six**, the truth made clear—
Life ripens deeper, year by year.

And if these years have taught me one
Enduring lesson, gently won,
It's not how long the journey's run,
But how much light we leave in one.

So here's to thirty-six—not end,
But wiser road and truer friend;
A heart still learning, still set free,
Forever becoming what it's meant to be.
